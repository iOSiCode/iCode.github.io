Why iCode

Simply because we do it elegently

December 8, 2020

  There are a handful of approches beginners tend to use to learn and improve their programming skills that can be catogerized in the following categories:

  • Books, which are complete and comprehensive, however, most books explain concepts out of context and present examples without a full working program.
  • Instructors, they are a better choice for beginners because they teach through projects; But from my experience with more than half a dozen of programming instructors, they use too much jargon and explain concepts in a language that sounds English but not quite English.
  • YouTube, which most beginners find more beginner friendly, however, there’s no strict plan and a huge amount of the resources are outdated.
  We at iCode look at programming from a fun and passionate point of view, that is both easy and more importantly understandable. Our goal is that at the end of the course, students look at programming the same way. You will learn how to code by making real iOS apps that can be installed on your iPhone, iPod or iPad. Concepts will be taught in the simplest ways with the help of examples that can be understood even by non-programmers. The course might seem shot but trust me this is just the start of something huge, and at the end of these two weeks you will be amazed of how much you have achieved.




OUTCOME

   - Solid understanding of iOS app development lifecycle.
   - Familiar with best programming practices and habbits.
   - Utilization of GitHub and Stack Overflow for future employment advantages.
   - Getting comfortable navigating and reading developer’s documentation.
   - Know how to solve problems that all software developers face.
   - And most importantly, learn how to become your own instructor.




Pre-rquisites

   - A Mac product (MacBook, Mac or iMac)
   - xCode – Free from the app-store
   - Discord – Free software (Download Discord)
   - Basic understanding of programming (Variables, if-statements, loops, functions)
        Note: You do not need to implement the concepts, just be familiar with them.
